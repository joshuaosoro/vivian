Introduction

Background of the Study

According to Ifeagwu et al. (2021), OOP, Donor funding and NHI are respectively the leading health financing mechanisms in sub-Saharan Africa.

The leading health financing mechanism in sub-Saharan Africa, OOP, faces major challenges; the leading being the fact that teenagers are mostly not employed as they have not attained the legal age of 18 years old. Consequently, teenagers face major challenges when it comes to relying on OOP as a health financing mechanism mainly because they don’t have the money to spend on their healthcare.

The National Health Insurance in Kenya (SHIF), previously called NHIF, established the Linda Mama Scheme with the principal aim of ensuring universal access to free maternity services for expectant mothers throughout pregnancy and childbirth Orangi et al. (2021). ….

Scope of the Study

This study will be carried out in the County of Siaya which comprises of six sub-counties namely: Alego, Usonga, Bondo, Ugenya, Ugunja, Gem and Rarieda.

Justification for the study.

KNBS (2014) reported that in Siaya county, 17.2% of the girls between the ages of 15 - 19 years old had begun child bearing. This figure is way higher compared to most of the other counties in Kenya. During a survey reported by the KNBS (2022), it was reported that 20.9% of the girls between the ages of 15 - 19 years old had begun child bearing; this of course was an increase thus bringing to the fore the need to ensure that teenage mothers are able to access conventional medication in Siaya county.

Definition of Terms

Teenager: Person aged between 13 - 19 years old.
